Overview

Metle is a personal project built from my own lived experience with music and mental health.. Designed to support emotional wellbeing through music reflection and intentional listening

Many music platforms focus on streaming and discovery but overlook the role music plays in supporting emotional wellbeing. There’s a gap in tools that help people reflect on their emotions and practice intentional listening as part of their mental health.

Research

People already turn to music to manage their emotions to unwind, feel understood, or boost their energy. While users often form strong emotional bonds with songs and artists, they don’t always have a way to reflect on or express the meaning behind those moments. There’s a growing desire for more intentional, mindful listening instead of just tuning in passively.

Ideate

I first compared leading platforms such as Spotify, Apple Music, and mental health–focused apps like Headspace and Calm. While Spotify and Apple Music dominate the streaming market and are beloved for their algorithms, branding, and features like Wrapped, they still treat music primarily as entertainment. None of these platforms directly support emotional regulation or mental health, nor do they encourage users to reflect on their wellbeing.

Russell’s Circumplex Model of Affect

To move beyond entertainment, I looked at psychology frameworks for understanding emotions. Russell’s Circumplex Model of Affect became a key influence, it organises emotions on two distinct axes: Energy (high ↔ low) and Mood (positive ↔ negative). When mapped together, these dimensions create a 2D emotional space often used in psychology to capture a fuller picture of how someone is feeling.

Image courtesy of ___________

This model directly inspired the creation of Metle’s Mood Map, where users can place themselves based on how they currently feel or how they want to feel, making music selection a more intentional and emotionally aware process.

From this blend of research and theory, I identified three core opportunities to reframe a streaming service into a platform that supports intentional listening and connects music directly to mental health.

Feature Goals

  • Mood Map — Create a 2D grid for selecting current or desired emotional states, making music selection more intentional and emotionally guided.
  • Journaling — Allow users to capture reflections tied directly to the music they’re listening to, encouraging mindful engagement with both emotions and songs.
  • Reflection Over Time — Provide a way to review and share past moods, music choices, and journal entries to uncover patterns and track emotional wellbeing over time.

User Flow

I created a visual user flow to map out the feature functionality before designing the interface. This also contributes to a smoother navigation experience, helping users accomplish tasks efficiently.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Blended

In the early stages of design, I explored additional features beyond the core Mood Map and Journaling. One of these was “Blended”, a tool that allowed users to mix up to three genres together and search for combinations, helping them discover more progressive and unexpected music.
While this idea leaned more toward music discovery than mental health, it reflected my goal of making listening more intentional and experimental. The feature later evolved into a focus on wellbeing, but it demonstrates how early exploration helped push the boundaries of what a streaming platform could offer.

Music Player

I also reimagined the music player itself. Spotify’s interface is clean, but I wanted mine to feel a little warmer. A bit more connected to the act of listening. That’s why I designed the player to visually incorporate a record/vinyl element not just as a design motif, but as a way to ground the user in the experience of music. It’s a subtle nod to physical music culture, but in a way that still fits within a digital, modern space.

Branding

For the visual identity, I created a logo that combines the letter M with the shape of a musical note, reinforcing Metle’s connection to both music and mental wellbeing. From there, I Intergrated the colours from the mood board. These colours became the foundation of the palette and were used to brighten the interface, giving it a clean, calm, yet vibrant personality.

The typography and overall layout choices were made to support this direction keeping the experience emotionally safe, approachable, and engaging, while still feeling fresh and expressive.

Reflection

In my research, I found that a common issue with portfolios is either being overloaded with information or too minimal, lacking enough detail. To solve this, I introduced a toggle that lets users choose the level of content they want to see. This keeps the experience immersive while also being respectful of the user's time. Another one of my core UX beliefs is that a portfolio should never be static. It should actively evolve. This portfolio revolves around receiving feedback, showing that I’m not just open to critique but actively inviting it in order to iterate and improve.
